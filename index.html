Where the Web was born Tim Berners-Lee, a British scientist, invented the World Wide Web (WWW) in 1989, while working at CERN. Although they typically spend some time on the CERN site, the scientists usually work at universities and national laboratories in their home countries. Reliable communication tools are therefore essential.The basic idea of the WWW was to merge the evolving technologies of computers, data networks and hypertext into a powerful and easy to use global information system.>
